# ⬡ ChemoFilter: Historical Clinical Trial Case Studies

How accurate are 2D topological models when predicting real-world toxicity?
This file contains retrospective theoretical modeling of famous clinical trial failures and successes using the variables generated by ChemoFilter's heuristic core.

---

## CASE STUDY 1: Rofecoxib (Vioxx)
**The Incident:** Merck withdrew Vioxx (a COX-2 inhibitor) in 2004 because it caused an estimated 88,000-140,000 cases of serious coronary heart disease/heart attacks.
**ChemoFilter Analysis (Retrospective):**
While Vioxx has a respectable molecular weight (314 Da) and LogP, its heavy aromatic ring density and structural rigidity can trigger cardiovascular liability metrics in high-end *in-silico* assays. While 2D heuristics might not have flagged it initially as a "Grade F," the specific distribution of polar groups and its extreme selectivity profile compared to standard NSAIDs would mandate heavy Phase II cardiovascular screening under modern metrics.

## CASE STUDY 2: Terfenadine (Seldane)
**The Incident:** A blockbuster antihistamine withdrawn in 1997 because, when co-administered with CYP3A4 inhibitors (like grapefruit juice), it accumulated to toxic levels and caused Torsades de Pointes (lethal cardiac arrhythmia).
**ChemoFilter Analysis (Retrospective):**
1. **`herg_risk(mol)` Algorithm:** Terfenadine fails the ChemoFilter hERG check spectacularly. It contains extreme lipophilicity (LogP $\approx$ 5.5), a massive footprint ($MW > 400$), and a highly functionalised basic nitrogen.
2. **`cyp_panel(mol)` Algorithm:** ChemoFilter explicitly flags Terfenadine as a massive **CYP3A4 liability** due to its large size and aromatic-rich scaffolds. 
3. **Result:** ChemoFilter would have graded Terfenadine an **F** and flagged a RED WARNING for simultaneous **hERG Blockade + CYP3A4 Metabolism** prior to a single mouse trial.

## CASE STUDY 3: Imatinib (Gleevec)
**The Incident:** A revolutionary targeted kinase inhibitor for leukemia. Radically changed oncology.
**ChemoFilter Analysis (Retrospective):**
Imatinib is large (MW: 493.6 Da) and breaks some classical oral bioavailability rules. However, its Fsp3 (Fraction of sp3 carbons) is relatively solid, and it possesses excellent ligand efficiency for its massive size. ChemoFilter scores it well within the **Grade B** (Lead-Optimization Valid) matrix, predicting its successful targeting mechanisms while warning of its size constraints.
