SECTION 1 — INTRODUCTION TO COMPUTATIONAL DRUG DISCOVERY
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
Traditional drug discovery is an arduous, high-risk, and capital-intensive process. On average, it takes 12 to 15 years and over $2.6 billion to bring a single new drug to the market. Despite these massive investments, approximately 90% of drug candidates entering clinical trials fail. Historically, the primary cause of failure was the lack of clinical efficacy. However, in the modern era, more than 50% of late-stage failures are attributed to poor ADMET (Absorption, Distribution, Metabolism, Excretion, and Toxicity) properties or clinical safety concerns.

This "ADMET bottleneck" occurs when a molecule shows high affinity for its target in a test tube but fails because it cannot reach the target in a human body or causes off-target toxicity. Computational drug discovery, or in silico screening, provides a solution by front-loading these safety checks. By identifying problematic molecules before the first wet-lab experiment, researchers can focus their resources on leads that are physically and biologically viable.

ChemoFilter was designed to serve as a comprehensive filtering vault for the computational chemist. It implements the most rigorous published rules and predictive models to scan chemical libraries up to 200 compounds in size. Developed within the context of the VIT Chennai Management Development Program (MDP) 2026, ChemoFilter represents the convergence of classical cheminformatics and generative AI narratives.


SECTION 2 — MOLECULAR REPRESENTATION
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
To process molecules computationally, they must be converted from 2D drawings into digital strings. ChemoFilter utilizes SMILES (Simplified Molecular Input Line Entry System) as its primary input format.

SMILES NOTATION
SMILES is a chemical notation that describes the structure of chemical species using short ASCII strings. Atoms are represented by their chemical symbols, and bonds are represented by punctuation.
- [C] is Carbon, [N] is Nitrogen, [O] is Oxygen.
- Branching is indicated with parentheses: CC(C)C is isobutane.
- Rings are indicated with numbers: C1CCCCC1 is cyclohexane.
- Aromaticity is indicated with lowercase: c1ccccc1 is benzene.

MORPHOLOGY & FINGERPRINTS
Beyond simple strings, ChemoFilter generates Extended-Connectivity Fingerprints (ECFP4 or Morgan Fingerprints). These are bit vectors that represent the local circular environment of every atom in the molecule. Fingerprints allow for the calculation of the Tanimoto Similarity Coefficient:
T = (A ∩ B) / (A ∪ B)
A Tanimoto score of 1.0 indicates identical structural environments (for a given radius), while a score of 0.0 indicates no shared features. ChemoFilter uses this to compare user leads against a built-in "Drug Atlas" of FDA-approved medicines.

SCAFFOLD DECOMPOSITION
ChemoFilter implements Bemis-Murcko Scaffold decomposition. This method strips away the side chains of a molecule and leaves only the ring systems and the linkers connecting them. In drug discovery, identifying the "core scaffold" is essential for understanding the structural diversity of a lead series.


SECTION 3 — PHYSICOCHEMICAL PROPERTIES
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
Molecular properties determine a drug's behavior as it navigates the complex environment of the human body.

MOLECULAR WEIGHT (MW)
Molecular weight is a fundamental indicator of a molecule's size. Orally active drugs typically have MW < 500 Da. Larger molecules tend to have lower intestinal permeability and are more difficult to transport across cell membranes.

LIPOPHILICITY (LOGP)
LogP is the octanol-water partition coefficient. It measures how "fat-loving" a molecule is. 
- High LogP (>5.0) molecules are poorly soluble in water and tend to bind non-specifically to proteins.
- Low LogP (<0) molecules are highly soluble but often fail to cross lipid cell membranes.
- The "Rule of Five" sweet spot is 0 to 5.

TOPOLOGICAL POLAR SURFACE AREA (TPSA)
TPSA measures the surface area of polar atoms (Oxygen, Nitrogen, and their attached Hydrogens). 
- TPSA < 140 Å² is required for good intestinal absorption.
- TPSA < 90 Å² is generally required for Blood-Brain Barrier (BBB) penetration.

FRACTION SP3 CARBON (FSP3)
Fsp3 is the ratio of sp3-hybridized carbons over the total carbon count. It measures the "three-dimensionality" or saturation of a molecule. Higher Fsp3 values often correlate with better solubility and a lower risk of clinical attrition, as they indicate more complex, non-flat structures.


SECTION 4 — DRUG-LIKENESS RULES
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
LIPINSKI RULE OF FIVE (Ro5)
Derived in 2001, the Rule of Five remains the most famous heuristic in drug discovery. It predicts that poor absorption or permeation is more likely when:
- There are more than 5 H-bond donors.
- The Molecular Weight is over 500.
- The LogP is over 5.
- There are more than 10 H-bond acceptors.

VEBER RULES
While Lipinski focuses on mass and lipophilicity, Veber identified that molecular flexibility and polar surface area are better predictors of oral bioavailability in rats:
- Rotatable bonds ≤ 10.
- TPSA ≤ 140 Å² (or sum of H-bond donors/acceptors ≤ 12).

ChemoFilter simultaneously evaluates molecules against Lipinski, Veber, Ghose, Egan, and Muegge filters, providing a comprehensive "Safety Grade" for each candidate.


SECTION 5 — ADMET PROPERTIES
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
A. ABSORPTION
Human Intestinal Absorption (HIA) is modeled using a combination of TPSA and lipophilicity. ChemoFilter utilizes the "BOILED-Egg" method by Daina et al. (2016), which plots WLOGP vs. TPSA to visualize the probability of absorption. Molecules falling within the white ellipsoid are predicted to have high gastrointestinal absorption.

B. DISTRIBUTION
The Blood-Brain Barrier (BBB) penetration is one of the most difficult properties to optimize. Drugs for the central nervous system (CNS) must cross the BBB (yellow yolk in the BOILED-Egg plot), while drugs for peripheral targets should ideally be excluded from the brain to avoid psychiatric side effects. Plasma Protein Binding (PPB) is also predicted, as only the "unbound" fraction of a drug is pharmacologically active.

C. METABOLISM
The Cytochrome P450 (CYP) system is the primary path for drug metabolism in the liver. ChemoFilter scans for interaction with five major isoforms:
- CYP3A4: Responsible for metabolizing ~50% of all drugs.
- CYP2D6: High genetic polymorphism, affecting antidepressants and opioids.
- CYP2C9, CYP2C19, and CYP1A2.
Inhibition of these enzymes can lead to dangerous drug-drug interactions (DDI).

D. TOXICITY
The hERG (human Ether-à-go-go-Related Gene) potassium channel is critical for cardiac repolarization. Inhibition of this channel by drugs can lead to "Long QT Syndrome" and sudden cardiac death—a major reason for post-market drug withdrawals. Ames mutagenicity is also flagged using structural alerts for potential carcinogens.


SECTION 6 — STRUCTURAL ALERTS AND SAFETY FILTERS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
PAINS (Pan-Assay Interference Compounds)
Some molecules are inherently "promiscuous." They may appear to be active in many biochemical assays but are actually just interfering with the readout (through metal chelation, redox activity, or aggregation). These "chemical con artists" are detected using 480+ SMARTS patterns defined by Baell and Holloway (2010).

ChemoFilter also implements the Brenk filter, which looks for reactive functional groups like epoxides, acyl halides, or nitro groups. While these groups are sometimes present in successful drugs, they are generally avoided in early-stage leads due to potential toxicity and instability.
